🍪 Cookie Policy

A plain-language explanation of what cookies Poetic Bytes uses, why each one exists, exactly what data it stores, and how you can control or delete every single one.

Effective: June 1, 2026 Last updated: June 2026 Solo operator GDPR & ePrivacy aware

The short version

Poetic Bytes uses four types of cookies: strictly necessary ones that keep the site working, analytics cookies (Google Analytics) that help me understand which lessons are useful, advertising cookies (Google AdSense) that fund the platform for free, and small functional items in localStorage that remember your preferences. You can opt out of the non-essential ones at any time — all lesson content stays fully accessible whether or not you accept cookies.

What Are Cookies?

A cookie is a small text file that a website places in your browser when you visit. Cookies are stored on your device and sent back to the originating website on subsequent visits. They are widely used to make websites work correctly, remember your preferences, and provide information to site owners.

Cookies are not programs and cannot execute code on your device. They cannot carry viruses or malware. They are simply text — key-value pairs of data.

Types by Origin

  • First-party cookies — set directly by Poetic Bytes. I set very few of these; most functional storage uses localStorage instead.
  • Third-party cookies — set by external services embedded in the site (Google Analytics, Google AdSense). These are the cookies that enable analytics and advertising.

Types by Duration

  • Session cookies — temporary cookies deleted automatically when you close your browser. They hold no data between visits.
  • Persistent cookies — remain on your device for a defined period (days, months, or years) or until you delete them. Analytics and advertising cookies are typically persistent.

Related Technologies

  • localStorage — a browser storage mechanism similar to cookies but stored only on your device and never sent to any server. I use this for UI preference storage.
  • Web beacons / pixels — tiny invisible images used to track page loads. Google Analytics uses these. I do not add any additional web beacons beyond what Analytics and AdSense set.
02

How I Use Cookies

I use cookies and localStorage for four purposes only. I have deliberately kept this to the minimum needed to run a free educational platform sustainably.

  • Making the site work — a small number of strictly necessary cookies ensure basic functionality.
  • Understanding usage — analytics cookies tell me which lessons people find most useful and where the curriculum can be improved.
  • Funding the site — advertising cookies enable Google AdSense to serve ads that pay for hosting and development time.
  • Remembering your preferences — localStorage items remember small UI choices (like whether you expanded the FAQ) so you do not have to repeat them each visit.

I do not use cookies for social media tracking, cross-site behavioural profiling beyond what AdSense requires, or building personal profiles linked to your identity.

03

Strictly Necessary Cookies

Strictly Necessary

Always On

These cookies are essential for the site to function correctly. Without them, parts of the site would break. I cannot offer you the option to disable these because the site simply would not work without them.

On Poetic Bytes, strictly necessary cookies are minimal because the site has no login system and no account features. The main strictly necessary storage comes from Vercel's edge infrastructure, which may set a session identifier to route requests correctly through its CDN.

  • These cookies contain no personally identifiable information.
  • They do not track you across other websites.
  • They are deleted when you close your browser or within a short session window.
04

Analytics Cookies

Google Analytics

Optional — Can Opt Out

I use Google Analytics to understand how people use Poetic Bytes. It tells me which lessons are visited most, how long people spend on each page, where in the world learners come from, and what devices they use. This information helps me prioritise which new content to write and how to improve existing lessons.

What Google Analytics does NOT do on this site:

  • It does not store your full IP address — I have enabled IP anonymisation, so only the first three octets are stored (e.g., 123.45.67.xxx).
  • It does not create an individually identified profile linked to your name or email.
  • It does not share data with me in a form that identifies you personally.

The analytics data I can see is aggregate — "3,000 people visited the Functions lesson this week" — not "John Smith from Manila visited at 3pm".

To opt out: install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

05

Advertising Cookies

Google AdSense

Optional — Can Opt Out

Poetic Bytes is entirely free to use. Google AdSense advertising is the only way I cover the costs of running this platform. AdSense sets cookies to serve ads and measure their performance.

How AdSense cookies work:

  • Contextual ads — AdSense reads the current page content to show relevant ads (e.g., a Python course ad on a Python tutorial page). This does not require any personal data.
  • Personalised ads — if you have not opted out, AdSense may also use your browsing history across the web (stored in Google's cookies) to show ads tailored to your interests.
  • Frequency capping — AdSense uses cookies to avoid showing you the same ad too many times.
  • Conversion measurement — AdSense tracks whether you clicked an ad and subsequently visited the advertiser's site.

I do not receive personally identifiable information about who clicks ads. I only see aggregate metrics — total impressions, click rates, and revenue.

To opt out of personalised ads: Google Ad Settings · Digital Advertising Alliance · Network Advertising Initiative.

Note on Ad Blocking

Using an ad blocker is your right and I respect it. If you find Poetic Bytes valuable and want to support it, consider disabling your blocker for this site only — it costs you nothing and directly helps me keep the content free and updated.

06

Functional Cookies & Local Storage

Functional / Preference Storage

Privacy-Safe

I use the browser's localStorage API — not traditional cookies — to store small UI preference values. Unlike cookies, localStorage data is never transmitted to any server. It lives only in your browser and is only readable by the Poetic Bytes domain.

  • FAQ expanded state — remembers which FAQ items you opened so they stay open when you scroll back up. Stored as a JSON array of section IDs. No personal data.
  • Newsletter dismissal — stores a flag (pb_newsletter_dismissed) so the subscription prompt does not reappear for 30 days after you dismiss it.
  • Code runner state — on lesson pages with the Pyodide code runner, the last code you typed is saved locally so you do not lose work if you accidentally refresh.

To clear this storage: open your browser's developer tools → Application (or Storage) tab → Local Storage → poetic-bytes.vercel.app → Delete All. Or simply clear your browser's site data for this domain.

Browser Controls — How to Manage Cookies

Every modern browser lets you view, block, and delete cookies. Here are the direct settings paths for the most popular browsers.

🌐
Google Chrome
Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data. Or: chrome://settings/cookies. You can block third-party cookies entirely or manage exceptions per site.
🦊
Mozilla Firefox
Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Manage Data. Firefox also offers Enhanced Tracking Protection (Standard, Strict, or Custom) which blocks third-party tracking cookies by default in Strict mode.
🧭
Apple Safari
Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data. Safari blocks cross-site tracking by default via Intelligent Tracking Prevention (ITP). You can also use "Prevent cross-site tracking" for stronger protection.
🔷
Microsoft Edge
Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data. Edge's "Tracking prevention" feature (Basic, Balanced, Strict) provides built-in third-party cookie blocking.
📱
Mobile Browsers (Android & iOS)
For Chrome on Android: Settings → Site settings → Cookies. For Safari on iOS: Settings app → Safari → Block All Cookies. For Firefox on Android: Settings → Privacy and Security → Enhanced Tracking Protection.

Impact of Disabling Cookies

Disabling analytics and advertising cookies has no effect on your ability to access any lesson content on Poetic Bytes. However, disabling all cookies may cause minor UI issues (e.g., your FAQ preferences not being remembered) and will prevent ads from loading — which reduces the revenue that keeps the platform free.

Opt-Out Tools

Beyond browser settings, these industry tools give you control over specific tracking technologies used on this site.

  • Google Analytics Opt-out Add-on — browser extension that prevents Google Analytics from collecting data on any site you visit. Download here.
  • Google Ad Settings — manage personalised ad preferences across all Google products, including AdSense. Manage at google.com/settings/ads.
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) — opt out of interest-based advertising from dozens of participating networks in one place. optout.aboutads.info.
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) — another industry opt-out tool covering multiple ad networks. optout.networkadvertising.org.
  • Your Online Choices (EU) — for users in Europe, the IAB Europe opt-out tool. youronlinechoices.eu.
  • Do Not Track (DNT) — I honour the DNT signal. When your browser sends a DNT:1 header, I do not load Google Analytics. Note: Google AdSense still loads as it is required to serve ads, but personalisation is suppressed.

Opting out through these tools sets an opt-out cookie in your browser. If you clear your cookies, you will need to opt out again. Some tools offer browser extensions that persist through cookie clears.
